What Is the Forgetting Curve?

German psychologist Hermann

Ebbinghaus wanted to understand

more about why we forget things

and how to prevent it. His

research produced the Forgetting

Curve – a visual representation of

the way that learned information

fades over time.

How to Prevent Forgetting and

Boost Your Memory

The most important discovery

Ebbinghaus made was that, by

reviewing new information at key

moments on the Forgetting Curve,

you can reduce the rate at which

you forget it!

This approach is often referred to

as "spaced learning" or

"distributive practice."
